# Types of Users on Research Africa

Research Africa supports multiple user types, each with specific roles and permissions within the platform. Below are the main user types and their typical responsibilities:

## 1. Journal Admin

-   **Description:** Manages all aspects of a specific journal.
-   **Permissions:**
    -   Add/edit/remove journal users
    -   Assign roles (editor, reviewer, author)
    -   Configure journal settings
    -   Oversee submissions and peer review

## 2. Editor

-   **Description:** Handles editorial workflow for submissions.
-   **Permissions:**
    -   Assign reviewers to manuscripts
    -   Make editorial decisions (accept, revise, reject)
    -   Communicate with authors and reviewers
    -   Manage editorial board

## 3. Reviewer

-   **Description:** Conducts peer review of assigned manuscripts.
-   **Permissions:**
    -   Access assigned manuscripts
    -   Submit reviews and recommendations
    -   Communicate with editors (if enabled)

## 4. Author

-   **Description:** Submits manuscripts to journals.
-   **Permissions:**
    -   Submit new manuscripts
    -   Track status of submissions
    -   Respond to editorial/reviewer feedback

## 5. Contributor

-   **Description:** May include guest editors, section editors, or other roles as defined by the journal.
-   **Permissions:**
    -   Varies by journal; may include limited editorial or review access

## 6. Publisher

-   **Description:** Responsible for final approval and publication of accepted articles.
-   **Permissions:**
    -   Access articles ready for publication
    -   Approve or reject final publication
    -   Manage publication schedule and release
    -   Communicate with editors and admins regarding publication status

## 7. Platform Super Admin

-   **Description:** Oversees the entire Research Africa platform (all journals).
-   **Permissions:**
    -   Manage all journals and users
    -   Access platform-wide settings and analytics
    -   Support and troubleshoot for all journals

---

**Note:**

-   Users can have multiple roles within the same journal (e.g., Editor and Reviewer).
-   Roles and permissions can be customized per journal as needed.

For more details on assigning roles and managing users, see the related documentation in the `docs` folder.
